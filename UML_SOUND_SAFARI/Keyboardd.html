KEYBOARD

Mobile and Tablet Users

Playing Sounds:

  • Tap the "PLAY" button corresponding to the instrument you wish to hear.
  • Each instrument has its own "PLAY" button.
  • To stop the sound, tap the "Stop" button next to the currently playing instrument.

Viewing Current Sound:

  • The message displayed at the bottom will update to indicate the currently playing sound or if no sound is playing.

Desktop and Laptop Users

Playing Sounds with Mouse Click:

  • Click on the "PLAY" button next to the instrument to play its sound.
  • Click the "Stop" button next to an instrument to stop the sound.

Playing Sounds with Keyboard Shortcuts:

Press the following keys on your keyboard to play respective sounds:

  • p: Piano
  • a: Accordion
  • h: Harmonium
  • r: Harpsichord
  • i: Pipe Organ
  • s: Synthesizer
  • m: Melodica
  • c: Celeste
  • u: Autoharp

Stopping Sounds:

  • Click the "Stop" button next to the instrument or press any other key other than which is not in the above list.

Viewing Current Sound:

  • The text displayed at the bottom will show the currently playing sound or indicate when no sound is playing.

Piano is a versatile and beloved keyboard instrument with a rich history dating back centuries. Its origins can be traced to early harpsichords of Europe, and its name is derived from the Italian word "pianoforte," meaning "soft-loud." The piano's complex mechanism allows for a wide dynamic range, from delicate pianissimos to powerful fortissimos, making it capable of expressing a vast array of emotions and musical styles. The piano's keyboard consists of 88 keys, arranged in seven octaves. The left hand plays the bass notes, while the right hand plays the treble notes. The pedals below the keyboard can be used to sustain notes, soften the sound, or add other effects. The piano is a popular instrument for both solo performances and ensemble playing. It is often used to accompany singers and other musicians, and it is a staple in classical, jazz, pop, and rock music. The piano's versatility and expressive capabilities have made it a beloved instrument for musicians and audiences alike.

Accordion is a versatile and portable keyboard instrument that is known for its unique sound and ability to provide both melody and harmony. It consists of a box-shaped body with a keyboard on one side and a set of bellows on the other. The bellows are pumped to create air pressure, which drives the reeds that produce the sound. The history of the accordion can be traced back to ancient civilizations, where early forms of bellows-operated instruments were used in various cultures. The modern accordion, with its distinctive shape and construction, emerged in Europe during the 19th century. The accordion is a popular instrument in a wide variety of musical genres, including folk, jazz, pop, and classical music. It is often used to provide accompaniment, melody, and harmony, and it is capable of producing a wide range of sounds. The accordion's versatility, portability, and unique sound have made it a beloved instrument for musicians and audiences alike.

Harmonium is a reed organ that is typically played with a keyboard and foot pedals. It produces a similar sound to the organ but is smaller and more portable. The harmonium has a box-shaped body with a keyboard on one side and a set of bellows on the other. The bellows are pumped to create air pressure, which drives the reeds that produce the sound. The history of the harmonium can be traced back to the 19th century, when it was first developed as a more portable and affordable alternative to the pipe organ. The harmonium quickly gained popularity in churches, homes, and schools. The harmonium is a versatile instrument that can be used to accompany singers and other musicians, or to play solo pieces. It is often used in religious music, folk music, and popular music. The harmonium's distinctive sound and portability have made it a beloved instrument for musicians and audiences alike.

Harpsichord is a keyboard instrument that produces a bright, plucking sound. It is similar to the piano in appearance, but it lacks the ability to sustain notes and has a more limited dynamic range. The harpsichord has a wooden case with strings that are plucked by quills or leather plectra attached to the ends of the keys. The history of the harpsichord can be traced back to the 14th century, when early forms of harpsichords were used in Europe. The harpsichord was a popular instrument during the Renaissance and Baroque periods, and it was often used to accompany singers and other musicians. The harpsichord is a versatile instrument that can be used in a variety of musical genres, including classical, baroque, and early music. Its distinctive sound and historical significance have made it a beloved instrument for musicians and scholars alike.

Pipe Organ is a large, complex musical instrument that produces sound through a series of pipes. It is typically played using a keyboard, which controls the flow of air through the pipes. The pipes are arranged in ranks, each with a different timbre and pitch. The history of the pipe organ dates back thousands of years, with evidence of organ-like instruments found in ancient civilizations. The modern pipe organ, with its complex system of pipes, keyboards, and stops, emerged in Europe during the Middle Ages. The pipe organ is a versatile instrument that is used in a wide variety of musical genres, including classical, sacred, and popular music. It is often used to accompany choirs and orchestras, or to play solo pieces. The pipe organ's powerful sound, majestic presence, and historical significance have made it a beloved instrument for musicians and audiences alike.

Synthesizer is an electronic musical instrument that can produce a wide range of sounds, from traditional instruments to completely synthetic tones. It consists of a keyboard and a variety of electronic components that generate, modify, and control sound. Synthesizers can be used to create a wide variety of sounds, including traditional instruments, abstract textures, and experimental effects. The history of synthesizers can be traced back to the early 20th century, when the first electronic instruments were developed. The development of synthesizers was greatly influenced by advances in electronic technology, such as the invention of the transistor and the integrated circuit. Synthesizers are used in a wide variety of musical genres, including electronic music, pop, rock, and classical music. They are often used to create unique soundscapes, experiment with new musical forms, and add a technological element to music. The versatility and expressive capabilities of synthesizers have made them a beloved instrument for musicians and producers alike.

Melodica is a small, portable keyboard instrument that is played by blowing into a mouthpiece and pressing the keys. It produces a similar sound to the accordion but is smaller and more lightweight. The melodica has a plastic body with a keyboard on one side and a mouthpiece on the other. The keys are connected to reeds that produce the sound when air is blown through the mouthpiece. The history of the melodica dates back to the 19th century, when it was first developed as a more affordable and portable alternative to the accordion. The melodica quickly gained popularity as a beginner's instrument and as a tool for music education. The melodica is a versatile instrument that can be used in a variety of musical genres, including folk, pop, and classical music. It is often used to accompany singers and other musicians, or to play solo pieces. The melodica's portability, affordability, and ease of use have made it a popular instrument for musicians of all ages and skill levels.

Autoharp is a small, portable stringed instrument that is played by plucking the strings with a metal bar. It has a rectangular body with a series of metal bars that run along the length of the instrument. These bars are connected to the strings, and when they are pressed down, they shorten the strings, changing the pitch. The history of the autoharp dates back to the 19th century, when it was first developed as a more affordable and portable alternative to the piano. The autoharp quickly gained popularity as a beginner's instrument and as a tool for music education. The autoharp is a versatile instrument that can be used in a variety of musical genres, including folk, pop, and country music. It is often used to provide accompaniment, melody, and harmony, and it is capable of producing a wide range of sounds. The autoharp's portability, affordability, and ease of use have made it a popular instrument for musicians of all ages and skill levels.
